Platforms resist backdoors

Leading privacy-focused platforms have publicly committed to resisting government demands for surveillance backdoors in their encrypted messaging services

Signal

Explicitly designed for zero access to user data. Founder Moxie Marlinspike has stated the company would exit markets that require weakening encryption rather than comply.

Telegram

CEO directly challenged EU's Chat Control proposal, threatening to leave the market rather than compromise user privacy with client-side scanning.

ProtonMail

Swiss-based service with zero-access encryption architecture. Has publicly committed to its "privacy by default" design principles regardless of regulatory pressure.
